The Capacity Crunch

Mathematical Modelling of Pandemic Logistics

1. The Context

A highly contagious virus is spreading in the city. The health department is racing to build capacity, but the virus is spreading exponentially.

This creates a race between two mathematical functions. The moment the virus function overtakes the capacity function is known as the Breaking Point.

2. Solving for System Failure

We need to find the specific week (t) where the number of patients (I) exceeds the number of beds (C).

Condition for Failure: $$ I(t) > C(t) $$

Where:

  • Infection (Exponential): \( I(t) = 200(2)^t \)
  • Capacity (Linear): \( C(t) = 800 + 400t \)

3b. Policy Paradox: Safety vs. Freedom

The government proposes a Strict Lockdown. This forces people to stay home, closing businesses and schools.

The Mathematical Impact: The infection rate slows down. Instead of doubling (base 2), cases grow more slowly (base 1.5).

$$ I_{lockdown}(t) = 200(1.5)^t $$

Look at how this changes the Breaking Point:

Time Infection (With Lockdown) Capacity Status
Week 4 1,012 2,400 SAFE
Week 5 1,518 2,800 SAFE
Week 6 2,278 3,200 SAFE
Week 7 3,417 3,600 SAFE

The Math Result: The lockdown buys us 4 extra weeks of safety! However, it requires forcing healthy people to lose their freedom and often their jobs.

The Question: Which position would you stand? Write a 50-100 word paragraph to explain your thoughts.

Position A

"The Greatest Good."

Argument: Look at the math of suffering. Does the suffering caused by unemployment and staying home outweigh the suffering of patients dying without hospital beds? We should choose the option that results in the least total pain for everyone.

Position B

"Individual Rights."

Argument: Focus on fairness and freedom. Does the government have the right to force a healthy person to lose their income just to protect a stranger? We argue that sacrificing individual freedom for the "group" is an abuse of power.
